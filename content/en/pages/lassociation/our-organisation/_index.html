The Réseau Excellence organization is closely linked to France's craft and industrial ecosystems, of which it is now a key player.

header_text: >- header_cta: display: false label: >- target: "" external: false contents_reading_time: seconds: 69 text: >- 1 minute contents: - kind: block template: chapter title: >- GENERAL ASSEMBLY ranks: self: 2 data: layout: no_background text: >-

The Ordinary General Meeting is held at least once a year and comprises all paid-up members of the association.

It deliberates on future orientations. It appoints or renews members of the Board of Directors. It also sets the annual membership fee.

After deliberation, the General Meeting votes on the annual report or activity report, and on the accounts for the financial year.

Decisions are taken by a majority of members present.

The association is managed by a Board of Directors made up of :

  • from 2 to 10 members who are directors of a living heritage company, elected for 2 years by the general meeting
  • 2 ex-officio members: a representative of external personalities, and the President (or his representative) of the Institut pour les savoir-faire français.

To join Réseau Excellence, you must :

  • have been awarded the "Entreprise du Patrimoine Vivant" (Living Heritage Company) label by the French government, or be, or have been, a member of the label award commission defined in article 4 of decree no. 2006-595 of May 23, 2006; or belong to a service or communications company involved in supporting EPVs;
  • have been approved by the Board of Directors;
  • adhere to the articles of association ;
  • and pay the membership fee set by the General Meeting.

The Board of Directors may refuse membership, giving reasons for its decision.

Membership fees are set each year by the General Meeting on the recommendation of the Board of Directors.
